## 9. Conviction, Stop-Loss, and Averaging Philosophy

This is one of the most nuanced sections of the session.

### Core message
In long-term investing, the instructor argues that the real protection is not always a tight stop-loss. Instead, it is:

- conviction
- thesis quality
- understanding the business
- knowing whether the fall is market-wide or company-specific

### His position
If a fundamentally strong company falls due to:
- macro panic
- broad market correction
- external event shock
- sentiment collapse unrelated to business breakdown

then averaging may make sense.

### But there is a critical qualifier
Averaging without understanding fundamentals is reckless.

You average:
- a strong company
- during non-thesis-breaking drawdowns
- when the business remains intact

You do **not** average:
- broken businesses
- thesis-invalidating collapses
- low-quality stocks just because price is lower

### Key teaching distinction
For investing:
- technical setup helps entry
- fundamentals justify holding
- conviction governs averaging
- blind averaging is gambling

This is not a minor point. It is one of the most important risk-management messages in the class.

## 10. Strategy 1 — Multi-Bagger Breakout (MBB)

## 10.1 Strategy Objective
Identify stocks that have spent years consolidating and are now attempting a structural breakout on the monthly timeframe.

## 10.2 Timeframe
- Primary chart timeframe: **Monthly**

## 10.3 Pattern Structure
The stock should exhibit:
- long-term sideways movement
- a clear consolidation range
- a visible upper boundary
- eventual breakout attempt above that boundary

## 10.4 Entry Logic
Entry is considered when:

- the stock closes **decisively above the high of the long consolidation range**
- confirmation is based on the **monthly close**
- not on an isolated daily spike or intramonth fake breakout

## 10.5 Why the instructor prefers monthly confirmation
Because a true structural breakout should reflect:
- institutional accumulation
- higher conviction
- lower noise
- greater regime-shift significance

A random daily move is not enough.

## 10.6 Stop-Loss Logic
The stop-loss is not described as a tight trader-style stop.

Instead, it is placed:
- below relevant prior swing structure
- under the consolidation logic
- far enough to respect long-term price behavior

## 10.7 Conceptual Logic
The thesis is that a multi-year base reflects prolonged absorption, and a monthly breakout from that base may signal the beginning of a major trend phase.

## 10.8 Important qualification
The instructor also says that technical breakout alone is not sufficient for long-term conviction. Fundamentals still matter and will be treated more directly in later sessions.

## 11. Strategy 2 — Monthly Bollinger Band Breakout Strategy (BB Strategy)

This is the most operationally defined strategy in the session.

## 11.1 Strategy Type
A **monthly investment-entry strategy** based on four simultaneous conditions.

## 11.2 Core Conditions
All four conditions must be satisfied.

### Condition 1 — Price Filter
- Daily close price must be **greater than or equal to ₹100**

Purpose:
- eliminate penny stocks
- avoid low-quality low-price noise
- focus on more tradable names

### Condition 2 — Liquidity Filter
- Daily traded volume must be **greater than or equal to 1,00,000 shares**

Purpose:
- ensure liquidity
- reduce slippage concerns
- avoid illiquid chart traps

### Condition 3 — Momentum Filter
- **Monthly RSI must be greater than or equal to 50**

Purpose:
- ensure the stock already has strength
- keep trades in names with bullish momentum context
- avoid weak charts trying to fake break out

### Condition 4 — Breakout Trigger
- **Monthly high must cross above the upper Bollinger Band**

Purpose:
- identify expansion phase
- capture strong breakout behavior
- combine structure and momentum

---

## 11.3 Entry Rule
The instructor says not to buy exactly at the raw trigger level.

Instead:
- place entry at **1% above the breakout level**

### Why
This acts as a buffer against:
- false pokes
- shallow fake breakouts
- marginal touches that do not sustain

---

## 11.4 Stop-Loss Rule
Although the strategy is based on monthly conditions, the stop-loss is read from a lower timeframe.

### Stop-loss basis
- recent **swing low**
- typically identified using the **daily chart**

### Logic
Monthly defines the setup.
Daily defines the invalidation.

This combination is practical:
- higher timeframe for idea
- lower timeframe for execution control

---

## 11.5 Exit / Trailing Rule
The instructor introduces **Super Trend** as the trailing mechanism.

### Rule concept
- remain in the trade while Super Trend remains favorable
- exit when Super Trend flips against the position

This means the strategy is not only about entry. It also has a structured hold/exit mechanism.

---

## 11.6 Evaluation Timing
This is critical.

The instructor states that the BB strategy should be evaluated only on the:

- **last trading day of the month**
- or after the monthly candle is effectively complete

### Why
Because mid-month:
- the candle is incomplete
- the band cross may disappear before close
- the signal may be invalidated by month-end

This is a strict anti-overtrading rule.

## 12. BB Strategy — Clean Rule Sheet

For implementation or documentation purposes, the BB strategy can be expressed as follows.

### Screening Conditions
A stock qualifies only if:

1. `daily_close >= 100`
2. `daily_volume >= 100000`
3. `monthly_rsi >= 50`
4. `monthly_high > monthly_upper_bollinger_band`

### Entry
- Buy at **1% above** breakout level

### Stop-Loss
- Place stop-loss at recent **daily swing low**

### Hold / Trail
- Hold while **Super Trend remains bullish**
- Exit when **Super Trend flips bearish**

### Review Frequency
- Review only on **month-end**
- No discretionary mid-month execution

## 13. Scanner-Based Workflow

The instructor is explicit that this system should not be run manually across thousands of stocks.

### Why a scanner is necessary
The number of tradable names is too high.
Manual checking is:
- slow
- error-prone
- emotionally biased
- operationally unsustainable

### Workflow described
1. use the predefined scanner
2. refresh on the last trading day of the month
3. inspect only the names surfaced by the scanner
4. apply the strategy rules exactly
5. ignore everything else

### Critical discipline rule
Do not take a trade if the stock does not meet all scanner conditions.

That means:
- no “almost qualifies”
- no “three out of four is enough”
- no “I like this company so I will bend the rule”
- no discretionary override

This is one of the strongest discipline messages in the class.
