## Teaching arc

### 1. Breakout Strategy (5 conditions)

First strategy of the day, built on the idea that a stock punching through resistance with conviction is a high-probability swing long:

1. **Big green candle** — visibly larger body than the surrounding candles.
2. **Body ≥ 70% of the candle range** — wick component small; rejection low.
3. **Volume > prior day's volume** — demand actually showed up.
4. **Delivery % ≥ 35–45%** — the move is not just intraday churn; real investors took delivery.
5. **Close ≥ 1% above resistance** — the same +1% buffer rule from Sessions 6–8; avoids false breakouts.

Execution:

- **Entry**: next-day open or confirmation close.
- **Stop-loss**: swing low / most recent daily higher-low.
- **False-breakout defence**: if close is within the 1% buffer, skip. Breakouts that fail the delivery-% filter are the most common retail trap.

### 2. BTST (Buy Today, Sell Tomorrow)

Dedicated short-duration setup for overnight holds:

- **Scanner source**: moneycontrol.com → *All Stats* → intersect **Top Gainers** and **Volume Shockers**.
- **Filter**: keep names where delivery percentage ≥ 45%.
- **Avoid** entries right before **Tuesday derivative expiry** windows — artificial volume distortion.
- **Exit**: next-day open or first strength.
- Framing: BTST is positioned as swing's shortest variant, not intraday. Used sparingly as a confluence trade when Nifty tailwind agrees.

### 3. Systematic vs Unsystematic risk, Alpha and Beta

Conceptual block before the next scanner-driven strategy:

- **Systematic risk** = market-wide, non-diversifiable (interest rates, geopolitics, RBI policy).
- **Unsystematic risk** = company-specific, diversifiable (management, sector, product).
- **Beta** measures systematic risk — how much a stock moves vs the market. Beta > 1 amplifies market moves; Beta < 1 dampens them.
- **Alpha** = stock return − market return. Alpha > 0 means the stock beat the benchmark after adjusting for market movement.
- Teaching point: a swing trader wants **high alpha, controlled beta** — outperformance without extreme volatility.

### 4. Alpha/Beta screener workflow (tickertip.in)

Practical application of the alpha/beta concept:

1. Open **tickertip.in** screener (the universe cited is ~5,739 NSE stocks).
2. Filter **Large Cap** to remove noise.
3. Apply **Alpha ≥ 15%**.
4. Apply **Beta between 0 and 1**.
5. Resulting shortlist is small (~22 names on the day of class).
6. Cross-check each name on the daily chart for trend structure before adding to the swing watchlist.

### 5. Trend Continuation Strategy (monthly RSI + Super Trend)

New swing setup combining Session-7's monthly filter with a new trailing indicator:

1. **Monthly filter**: monthly RSI ≥ 60 (same as Buying-in-the-Dips).
2. **Daily filter**: daily close **above Super Trend** (green).
3. **Entry**: green candle confirming the above; buffered +1%.
4. **Stop-loss**: swing low on daily.
5. **Trail**: Super Trend line itself.

On-screen examples: **ITC, SBI, BEL/BIL**. Framed as a lower-maintenance variant of Buying-in-the-Dips — the Super Trend handles trailing so the trader doesn't need to re-draw swing structure each week.

### 6. 13/34 SMA + 200 SMA strategy

Classic moving-average crossover adapted to swing:

- **200 SMA** = long-term trend anchor. Approximately **314 trading days**; stocks above 200 SMA are in a primary uptrend.
- **13 SMA (fast)** crossing above **34 SMA (slow)**, both above the 200 SMA = swing long.
- Entry on the crossover candle; SL swing low; trail with 34 SMA or swing structure.
- On-screen example: **Infosys**, walked through with a single-trade annualised figure cited around **67% over ~30 days** as framing.

### 7. 44 SMA strategy

Single-MA variant for stocks that trend cleanly along one average:

- Condition: daily close above **44 SMA** in a primary uptrend.
- Reversal signal: price reclaiming the 44 SMA after a pullback.
- Example: **Glottis Limited** walked through as a clean-trending mid-cap.

### 8. 9/15 EMA + Super Trend on 4-hour chart

Shorter-timeframe swing / fast-swing variant:

- Timeframe: **4-hour** candles.
- Entry: green candle where price is above both **9 EMA and 15 EMA** and the **Super Trend** is green on the same 4h chart.
- Stop-loss: recent 4h swing low.
- Notes: chartlink.com scanner mentioned as the source for surfacing candidates.

### 9. Mutual-fund-driven stock discovery (trendline.com)

Fundamental sourcing method used in parallel with the technical scanners:

- Tool: **trendline.com** — lists fund portfolios.
- Process: study **flex-cap and thematic funds** (PSU, infrastructure, solar, EV) and extract their **top holdings**.
- The assumption: if a competent fund manager allocated 3–5% of a large fund into a name, the fundamental work is already done — the swing trader only needs to pick the technical entry.
- On-screen example: **HDFC Flexi Cap** top holdings.
- Framing aside: Warren Buffett's **A-to-Z company exercise** — if you can't name a company for each letter of the alphabet, you don't know the universe well enough yet.

### 10. Algo trading sidebar

- Typical retail-algo accuracy cited at **60–70%**.
- Main benefit: removes emotion from execution.
- Instructor's preference: manual **techno-funda** (technical + fundamental confluence) over pure algos, because the edge comes from reading context the algo doesn't see.
- **NISM Research Analyst** exam mentioned as the right next certification for students who want to turn the class into a career.
